NOW, THEREFORE, in acknowledgment of the mutual promises and commitments within this agreement, the Parties agree to the following terms:

I. Definition.

The information under this Agreement, identified or deemed Confidential by the Disclosing Party, regardless of whether it is shared before or after the date of this Agreement, shall include, but is not limited to, the following:

Any and all information of either Party that is disclosed, furnished, or communicated, whether technical, business, or otherwise (including, but not limited to, trade secrets, know-how, and information related to discoveries, instructions, ideas, inventions, concepts, software, equipment, designs, drawings, specifications, techniques, processes, systems, models, data, source code, object code, documentation, diagrams, flow charts, research, development, business plans or opportunities, business strategies, marketing plans or opportunities, future products or opportunities, products or opportunities under consideration, procedures, and any information related to finances, costs, prices, suppliers, vendors, customers, and employees) which are disclosed by either Party or on its behalf, whether before, on, or after the date of this Agreement, regardless of form (whether in writing, machine-readable form, text, drawings, photographs, graphics, designs, plans, or any other form and marked by an appropriate stamp or legend as Confidential, or if disclosed orally, identified as confidential at the time of disclosure or implied by the context and subsequently documented in writing and delivered to the Receiving Party within thirty days of disclosure) by or on behalf of the Disclosing Party to the Receiving Party through the Receiving Party’s directors, officers, employees, representatives, or agents (collectively referred to as “Representatives”), as well as any copies thereof; and the fact that discussions are taking place between the Parties.

Notwithstanding any other provision of this Agreement, the parties acknowledge that Confidential Information does not include any information that:

  • is or becomes publicly accessible without violating this Agreement;
  • was already in the possession of the Receiving Party and was not obtained directly or indirectly from the Disclosing Party, as demonstrated by written records;
  • is lawfully received by a Party from a third party without any confidentiality obligations; and
  • is required to be disclosed by law.

Marketing and Development Information - This encompasses the Disclosing Party's plans related to marketing strategies or business growth, including costing, policies, quotations, forecasts, and strategic initiatives.

Business Operations Information - This pertains to details about the business operations of the Disclosing Party, such as workforce numbers, financial activities, and information regarding vendors, providers, and partners not disclosed to the public.

Product Information - This includes specifications for products created by the Disclosing Party, as well as products resulting from or associated with projects undertaken for the Disclosing Party or its clients, or details of products in the research and development phase.

Manufacturing and Production Processes - This refers to identified processes and systematic methods used to create product outputs, such as manufacturing or production. It includes, but is not limited to, formulas, technical procedures, methodologies, specifications, devices, equipment, designs, models, or software files utilized in manufacturing. This may also refer to Proprietary Information.

Service Information - This covers information about services offered by the Disclosing Party, such as manpower scheduling, training, and maintenance.

Intellectual Property - This includes creations of the mind, such as inventions, designs, symbols, names, and images developed for commerce. Examples include logos, computer code and algorithms (both human-readable and machine-readable), reports, technical designs, database structures, charts, and document design specifications, as well as music subject to copyright protection.

Technology - This pertains to technical and scientific information and materials created by the Disclosing Party that have not been publicly disclosed. It includes the use of any machine, appliance, components, and documented scientific processes.

Accounting Information - This involves financial reports, worksheets, balance sheets, undisclosed assets and liabilities, inventories, payroll, and any other financial-related information not disclosed or shared without the Disclosing Party's consent.

Confidential Information may also include any information provided by a third party to the Disclosing Party under a non-disclosure agreement, which the Receiving Party may acquire accidentally, incidentally, or otherwise, whether directly or indirectly.

II. Obligations.

In consideration of the disclosure and release of Confidential Information by or on behalf of the Disclosing Party to the Receiving Party, the Receiving Party agrees to maintain the strictest confidentiality regarding all such Confidential Information as defined in this Non-Disclosure Agreement.

The Receiving Party commits to using the Confidential Information exclusively for the purpose of {{purposeOfTheWork}}.

The Receiving Party shall take all necessary steps and measures to reduce the risk of disclosure, dissemination, or unauthorized use of the Confidential Information. This includes ensuring that only Representatives whose responsibilities require access to the Confidential Information are granted such access on a "need-to-know" basis. Before sharing any Confidential Information with these Representatives, the Receiving Party must obtain a written acknowledgment from them, affirming their agreement to adhere to all restrictive covenants and confidentiality obligations outlined in this Agreement. The Receiving Party remains accountable for any violations of this Agreement by its Representatives and must take all reasonable measures (including but not limited to legal action) to prevent such Representatives from engaging in unauthorized disclosure or use of the Confidential Information.

The Receiving Party shall ensure that the Confidential Information is not copied or reproduced in any form whatsoever by itself, its Representatives, or any third parties without the explicit written consent of the Disclosing Party. The Receiving Party acknowledges and agrees that the ownership and copyright of the Confidential Information disclosed to it are vested in the Disclosing Party.

The Receiving Party shall further ensure the security and control of any Confidential Information, whether in documentary or other tangible or intangible forms. This includes physically restricting access to such Confidential Information to secure areas within the Receiving Party's premises and storing it in a manner that prevents unauthorized access.

The Receiving Party agrees to promptly return to the Disclosing Party all Confidential Information upon the Disclosing Party's request at any time.

The Receiving Party shall not, nor shall it permit any of its Representatives to, publish any press releases, make announcements, or issue any denials or confirmations in any medium regarding any information covered by this Agreement, without the explicit written consent of the Disclosing Party.

At all times, it is understood that no liability for any breaches by the Company and/or its Representatives shall be attributed to the shareholders of the Company or any of its affiliated, related, associated, or subsidiary group companies, or their shareholders, in any manner whatsoever.

III. Exclusions From Confidential Information

The Receiving Party shall not require the Disclosing Party's consent to share, disclose, or copy the Confidential Information in the following scenarios:

  1. The Confidential Information is already publicly available at the time of disclosure;
  2. The Receiving Party was already aware of the information prior to its disclosure;
  3. The Receiving Party has lawfully obtained the Confidential Information from a third party not under any obligation or agreement with the Disclosing Party;
  4. The Confidential Information has been independently developed by the Receiving Party without reliance on the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information.

IV. Disclosure Due to Court Order/Governmental Action

If the Receiving Party or any of its Representatives is compelled to disclose any Confidential Information by court order or governmental directive, the Receiving Party shall immediately provide written notice to the Disclosing Party. This notice allows the Disclosing Party the opportunity to object to the disclosure and/or seek a protective order or other remedy. If the Disclosing Party cannot successfully object or obtain a remedy, the Receiving Party or its Representative(s) shall disclose only the Confidential Information required by the court order or directive and will make every effort to ensure confidential treatment of the disclosed information.

V. Non-Compete

Both parties acknowledge the importance of their respective relationships, including knowledge of their clients, partners, channels, programs, initiatives, and subsidiaries. The Receiving Party agrees not to bypass or undermine this relationship directly or indirectly with any third party without the Disclosing Party's written consent.

Furthermore, the parties agree not to engage in direct or indirect competition with the other party’s clients, affiliates, or associated work engagements without prior written approval.

VI. Reporting Unauthorized Disclosure, Misappropriation, or Misuse of Confidential Information

The Receiving Party shall immediately notify the Disclosing Party of any unauthorized disclosure, misappropriation, or misuse of Confidential Information as soon as they become aware of such incidents.

VII. Term of the Agreement

For a period of five (5) years following the termination of this Agreement, both parties shall treat all information as confidential and take reasonable measures to prevent unauthorized disclosure. The parties shall ensure their employees, agents, and assistants understand and comply with the terms of this Agreement to uphold confidentiality.

Upon the termination or completion of the Agreement, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all materials and documents containing Confidential Information, including summaries, copies, and excerpts stored in any format. The destruction must be verified in writing.

VIII. Return of Confidential Information

Upon the termination of this Agreement or at the request of the Disclosing Party, all Confidential Information disclosed under this Agreement shall be returned. Physical records, hardcopy materials, and other tangible forms of Confidential Information shall be delivered to the Disclosing Party. Digital records must be deleted with no copies retained by the Receiving Party.

If delivery is not feasible, the parties may agree to destroy the Confidential Information. Such destruction must be certified in writing and witnessed by an authorized representative of the Disclosing Party or a duly authorized officer of the Receiving Party.

IX. Ownership

This Non-Disclosure Agreement is non-transferable and requires the written consent of both parties for any transfer.

Each party retains exclusive ownership of its own Confidential Information, except as stated in the Exclusions from Confidential Information clause. No rights to the other party's Confidential Information, by license or otherwise, shall be granted without explicit consent. Any recommendations or contributions made by the Receiving Party and incorporated into the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information shall be considered part of the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information.

X. Rights Cumulative/Entirety/Variation/Severability

The rights and remedies of each Party outlined herein are cumulative and not exclusive of any rights or remedies granted by law to that Party.

This Agreement represents the entire agreement between the Parties and overrides all previous agreements related to its subject matter.

No modification of this Agreement will be valid unless it is documented in writing and signed by the Parties (or their authorized representatives).

If any provision of this Agreement (or a portion of any provision) is determined by a court or other competent authority to be invalid, illegal, or unenforceable, that provision or portion thereof shall, to the extent necessary, be considered as not forming part of this Agreement and shall be severed accordingly. The validity and enforceability of the remaining provisions will not be affected.

XI. Assignment

Neither Party may assign or delegate any of its rights or transfer any of its obligations under this Agreement without obtaining the prior written consent of the other Party.

XII. General

All amendments or additions to this Agreement must be documented in writing and signed by both Parties. The effective date of this Agreement shall coincide with its execution date.

XIII. Warranties

The Disclosing Party provides no warranties or representations to the Receiving Party regarding the accuracy, completeness, or performance of the Confidential Information. No Party shall be held liable for the use of the Confidential Information.

XIV. Injunctive Relief

The Parties agree that in the event of a breach resulting from careless handling of Confidential Information, the aggrieved Party shall have the right to seek injunctive relief to prevent further damage or harm, which could lead to irreparable consequences.

XV. Counterparts

The Parties may sign this Agreement in multiple counterparts, each of which will be considered an original. Together, they shall constitute one and the same document.

XVI. Entire Agreement

This Agreement is recognized as the complete understanding between the Parties and supersedes any prior discussions or agreements regarding its subject matter. Any amendments, modifications, or alterations to this Agreement require mutual consent and must be signed by both Parties.

XVII. Separability Clause

If any provision of this Agreement is declared invalid by a competent court, such invalidity will apply solely to that specific provision. The remaining provisions will remain in full force and effect.

XVIII. Governing Laws

This Agreement shall be interpreted and enforced under the laws of the United States, excluding other state laws and without regard to conflicts of laws principles.

Any disputes arising from this Agreement shall be brought before the courts of the United States.

Each Party consents to the jurisdiction of these courts and waives any objections regarding jurisdiction, venue, or the convenience of the forum.
