II. Confidential Information

"Confidential Information" refers to all proprietary, non-public, or sensitive information shared between the parties during the course of their commercial relationship. This includes, but is not limited to, business strategies, financial data, technical processes, software, designs, research, and any other materials or knowledge that may give one party a competitive advantage. Confidential Information also includes information received from third parties under confidentiality obligations. Information that is publicly available, obtained through legal means independent of this agreement, or required to be disclosed by law or a court order, is not considered Confidential Information.

III. Protection of Confidential Information

Both parties agree to exercise the utmost care in safeguarding Confidential Information and to take all reasonable measures to prevent unauthorized access, use, or disclosure. The Confidential Information should be protected with at least the same level of security and caution as each party applies to its own proprietary information, but no less than a reasonable standard of care. Any disclosure of Confidential Information to employees, agents, consultants, or subcontractors will only be permitted when absolutely necessary for the performance of the agreement, and such individuals must be bound by confidentiality obligations equivalent to those in this Agreement. Both parties will be held responsible for ensuring that these individuals adhere to the terms of this Agreement.

IV. Violation of this Agreement

In the event of a violation or breach of this Agreement, the party aware of the breach must promptly notify the other party in writing. The notice must include details regarding the nature and extent of the breach. The affected party reserves the right to pursue any and all legal remedies available, including but not limited to seeking injunctive relief, specific performance, or damages for any losses incurred as a result of the breach. The breaching party shall be liable for any direct or indirect harm caused by the unauthorized disclosure or misuse of Confidential Information.

V. Return of Materials Containing Confidential Information

Upon the termination or expiration of this Agreement or the commercial relationship, each party is obligated to return all materials, documents, files, or any other items containing Confidential Information, whether in physical or electronic form. The return of materials must occur within a reasonable timeframe, or if requested, such materials must be destroyed, with proof of destruction provided to the other party. This obligation to return or destroy materials containing Confidential Information applies even after the end of the relationship or the termination of this Agreement, ensuring that no party retains access to sensitive information without authorization.

Disclosure of Confidential Information to any third party, or the use of such information for any purpose not expressly authorized by this Agreement, is strictly prohibited. No party shall disclose any part of the Confidential Information to any individual, entity, or organization without obtaining prior written consent from the other party, except when required by law or governmental order. Unauthorized disclosure or use of Confidential Information will result in immediate legal action and potential compensation for damages.
